Fish Pie

Description

Creamy, comforting, and packed with tender chunks of fish, fish pie is a classic British dish that brings warmth to any dinner table. Traditionally made with a medley of white fish, smoked haddock, and sometimes salmon, it's gently poached in a flavorful white sauce before being topped with a buttery mashed potato crust. The result is a rich, savory bake with just the right balance of texture and taste.

Whether you're feeding a crowd or just craving something hearty, fish pie is a reliable go-to. It’s endlessly adaptable—toss in some peas or hard-boiled eggs, switch up the fish, or add a splash of cream for extra indulgence. This recipe keeps things simple and family-friendly, with straightforward steps and plenty of opportunity to make it your own.

Ingredients

  • White fish
  • Potatoes
  • Butter
  • Onions
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Prepare the fish: Cut your fish (such as cod, smoked haddock, and salmon) into chunks and poach gently in milk with a bay leaf and a few peppercorns until just cooked. Remove the fish and set aside, reserving the milk.
  2. Make the white sauce: In a saucepan, melt butter and stir in flour to make a roux. Gradually whisk in the reserved poaching milk, cooking until thickened. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg if you like.
  3. Assemble the filling: Gently fold the cooked fish into the sauce along with any extras like peas, chopped parsley, or sliced boiled eggs. Transfer the mixture to a baking dish.
  4. Top with mash: Spread prepared mashed potatoes evenly over the fish filling, fluffing the top with a fork for a crispier texture once baked.
  5. Bake and serve: Bake in a preheated oven at 200°C (180°C fan) for about 25–30 minutes, or until the top is golden and the filling is bubbling. Let it rest briefly before serving.
