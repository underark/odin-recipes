Fish and Chips

Description

Fish and chips is a classic British comfort food that pairs flaky white fish—traditionally cod or haddock—dipped in a crisp, golden batter with thick-cut, fluffy-on-the-inside chips (fries). The fish is deep-fried until perfectly crunchy, while the chips are either double-fried or oven-roasted for maximum texture. This hearty dish is typically served with a wedge of lemon, a sprinkle of salt and malt vinegar, and often accompanied by sides like mushy peas or tartar sauce.

Beloved across the UK and beyond, fish and chips is more than just a meal—it's a nostalgic experience often tied to seaside towns, paper-wrapped takeaways, and Friday night rituals. The secret to getting it just right lies in a cold, fizzy batter (often with beer or soda water) and properly drained oil to keep things light, not greasy. Whether homemade or grabbed from the local chippy, it's a satisfying blend of crunch, tenderness, and savory indulgence.

Ingredients

  • Cod
  • Batter
  • Potatoes
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Prep the potatoes: Peel and cut potatoes into thick chips. Soak in cold water for at least 30 minutes to remove excess starch, then drain and pat dry thoroughly.
  2. Make the batter: Whisk together flour, baking powder, salt, and cold beer or sparkling water until you get a smooth, thick batter. Chill the batter in the fridge while you work on the chips.
  3. Fry the chips (first round): Heat oil to 160°C (320°F) and fry the chips in batches for 4–5 minutes, until soft but not browned. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. Fry the fish: Heat the oil to 180°C (350°F). Pat the fish fillets dry, dust lightly with flour, then dip in the batter and fry until golden and crisp—about 5–7 minutes. Drain on a rack or paper towels.
  5. Finish the chips: Return the chips to the oil at 180°C (350°F) and fry again until golden and crispy, about 2–3 minutes. Serve everything hot with lemon wedges, vinegar, and your favorite sides.
